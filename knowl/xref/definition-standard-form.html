Definition 2.7.2. Standard Form.

It is always possible to write an equation for a line in the form
\begin{equation} Ax+By=C\tag{2.7.1} \end{equation}
where \(A\text{,}\) \(B\text{,}\) and \(C\) are three numbers (each of which might be \(0\text{,}\) although at least one of \(A\) and \(B\) must be nonzero). This form of a line equation is called standard form. In the context of an application, the meaning of \(A\text{,}\) \(B\text{,}\) and \(C\) depends on that context.
