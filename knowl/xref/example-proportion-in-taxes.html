Example 1.1.16.

Property taxes for a residential property are proportional to the assessed value of the property. A certain home is assessed at \(\$234{,}100\) and its annual property taxes are \(\$2{,}518.92\text{.}\) Set up an equation to find the annual property taxes for the house next door that is assessed at \(\$287{,}500\text{.}\)
🔗
Explanation.
Let \(T\) be the annual property taxes (in dollars) for a property assessed at \(\$287{,}500\text{.}\) We can write and this proportion:
\begin{align*} \frac{\text{tax}}{\text{property value}}\amp=\frac{\text{tax}}{\text{property value}}\\ \frac{2518.92}{234100}\amp=\frac{T}{287500} \end{align*}
