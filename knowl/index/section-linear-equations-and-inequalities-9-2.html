Consider an equation with fractions present in the coefficients or terms, for example:
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{4}x+\frac{2}{3}=\frac{1}{6} \end{equation*}
We could solve this using the same steps from earlier in this section, but we’d have to do some fraction subtraction, followed by some fraction division. In other words, we’d need to do fraction arithmetic, which ismore likely to lead to human error than whole number arithmetic.
