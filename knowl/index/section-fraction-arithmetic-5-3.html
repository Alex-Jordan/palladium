We know that dividing something by \(2\) is the same as multiplying it by \(\frac{1}{2}\text{.}\) This works in the other direction too: dividing by \(\frac{1}{2}\) is the same as multiplying by \(\frac{2}{1}\text{,}\) or just \(2\text{.}\) The more general property is that dividing by \(\frac{a}{b}\) is equivalent to multiplying by the reciprocal \(\frac{b}{a}\text{.}\)
