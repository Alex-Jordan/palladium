The most basic technique for factoring is recognizing the greatest common factor, which is the largest factor that divides into several terms without involving fractions. For example, the greatest common factor between \(6\) and \(8\) is \(2\text{,}\) since \(2\) divides nicely into both \(6\) and \(8\) and no larger number can do that.
